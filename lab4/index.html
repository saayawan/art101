Lab 4 - Pseudocode

Challenge

The challenge of this lab was to practice thinking in terms of pseudocode before starting actual programming. We translated real-life tasks and a simple game into structured outlines, then turned that into JavaScript comments.

Problems

We weren't sure how to organize the game logic at first or how detailed to go with our tasks. Clarifying what counts as a high-level vs. low-level step helped us move forward.

Reflection

This lab helped us understand how to plan before coding. Writing in structured pseudocode made it easier to think logically and break down complex actions into manageable parts.

Results

Task 1: Pseudocode - How to Build a Custom Mechanical Keyboard

  • Choose Parts
    • Decide on layout (60%, TKL, full-size)
    • Choose PCB
    • Select switches (tactile, linear, clicky)
    • Choose keycaps
    • Pick case and plate material
    • Choose stabilizers
  • Prepare Components
    • Lube switches (optional)
    • Clip and lube stabilizers
    • Install stabilizers
    • Insert switches
  • Assemble Keyboard
    • Mount switches
    • Solder (if needed)
    • Place PCB and plate into case
    • Screw everything in
  • Test Functionality
    • Plug in and use keyboard tester
    • Fix any errors
  • Customize and Finish
    • Install keycaps
    • Flash firmware (QMK/VIA)
    • Customize RGB/layout
